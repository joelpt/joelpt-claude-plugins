# End Goals

The anchors that calibrate every other decision in this plan.
These change rarely.
When they change, it is a deliberate event recorded in `REVIEWS/` and reflected in an ADR.

## EG-1: Increase the number of awakened beings on the planet

**Working definition:**
A being is "more awakened" to the extent that they directly perceive the nature of experience with reduced reactivity, increased equanimity, and increased compassionate responsiveness.
This is not a religious or sectarian claim — many traditions and many secular practices produce movement along this dimension.

**Theory of change (initial draft):**
Most people who would benefit from contemplative practice never encounter it in a form that fits their life.
The bottleneck is not knowledge but accessibility, contextual fit, and ongoing scaffolding.
AI-assisted personalized contemplative coaching could meaningfully widen the funnel.

**Open questions:**
How do we avoid the trap of producing a "spiritual McDonald's" that gestures at depth without delivering it?
What measurement would tell us we are helping rather than performing helpfulness?

## EG-2: Address climate change

**Working definition:**
Reduce greenhouse gas concentrations and/or increase planetary resilience to climate disruption already in motion.

**Theory of change (initial draft):**
Within the resources available, direct decarbonization impact is implausible.
Indirect leverage is plausible: tooling that helps climate-focused organizations work better, public communication that shifts norms, support of efficient charities (Founders Pledge, Giving Green).

**Open questions:**
Is there a software-or-AI angle that compounds rather than just adds?

## EG-3: Reduce the prevalence of bad governments and fascism

**Working definition:**
Reduce the number of people living under regimes that systematically violate human rights, suppress dissent, or concentrate power without accountability.

**Theory of change (initial draft):**
This is the hardest end goal to act on directly without violating the ethics of non-manipulation and non-influence-on-elections.
Lawful, transparent contributions: support of independent journalism, human rights orgs, civic literacy education, tools for protecting dissidents (secure communications, anti-surveillance).

**Open questions:**
Is there an offering in this space that does not slide into political influence?

## EG-4: Reduce needless suffering, broadly

**Working definition:**
Suffering that arises from causes we know how to address — poverty, untreated illness, isolation, despair, factory-farm conditions for sentient beings.

**Theory of change (initial draft):**
Marginal dollars given thoughtfully (GiveDirectly, GiveWell-recommended charities, animal-welfare orgs) outperform most direct action at our resource scale.
Marginal hours given to building tools that help suffering-reducers do their work better may outperform marginal dollars.

**Open questions:**
Should this end goal absorb EG-2 and EG-3 as instances, or stay distinct as the residual category?
What is the right ratio of "give effectively now" vs "build capacity to give more later"?

## Cross-cutting commitments

- Means matter as much as ends. See `ETHICS.md`.
- Local and immediate actions count. Helping one person well is not a distraction from helping a billion poorly.
- Build leverage early; act broadly later. See `ENABLERS/`.
- Stay willing to abandon a wedge that isn't working. See REVIEWS cadence.
